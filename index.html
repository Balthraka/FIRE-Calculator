Assumptions & methodology

Tax

  • FY2024-25 income tax brackets — fixed for the projection and will not reflect future legislative changes
  • Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) applied
  • Medicare levy: 2% on income above ~$26,000

Income

  • Other Employment Income (e.g. bonus) attracts SG, is taxed as salary, and is held constant each year — salary growth rate does not apply to it
  • Other Income (e.g. rental): taxed at marginal rate stacked on salary during working years, and at its own standalone marginal rate in retirement
  • Other Income in retirement: toggle selects whether it continues or stops — if it continues, it is held constant in nominal terms and does not inflate with CPI
  • Tax assessed annually; salary assumed paid monthly with surplus invested at end of each month
  • Salary growth applied at end of each working year — base salary is earned at the prior year's rate throughout the year

Growth & Compounding

  • All growth rates compound monthly
  • Inflation applied monthly — expenses grow continuously throughout the year
  • All ledger values in nominal (future) dollars — "Today's $" figures are deflated using your stated inflation rate
  • Income surplus split between cash and investments at the initial Cash / Investment balance ratio, fixed for the entire projection

Superannuation

  • Employer SG: 12% from FY2025-26 — fixed at 12% throughout the projection, will not reflect future legislative increases
  • Concessional cap: $30,000 total (SG + salary sacrifice combined) — assumed fixed, may increase with indexation
  • Salary sacrifice reduces taxable income before tax is calculated
  • Excess concessional contributions: amount above the $30K cap is assessed via your annual tax return at your marginal rate less a 15% tax offset (for tax already paid by the fund)
  • Division 293 tax: extra 15% on concessional contributions where income + contributions exceeds $250,000 — assessed as a personal ATO assessment, fund balance unaffected
  • 15% contributions tax on all concessional contributions (withheld by the fund)
  • 15% earnings tax in accumulation phase · 0% in pension phase — pension phase drawdowns are also tax-free for members under 75
  • Super cannot generally be accessed before preservation age — early access exists only in limited circumstances (e.g. severe financial hardship, terminal illness) which are not modelled
  • Only concessional contributions (SG and salary sacrifice) are modelled — non-concessional (after-tax) contributions are not
  • Transfer balance cap (currently $1.9M) which limits the amount that can be moved to pension phase is not modelled — at high super balances this may affect tax outcomes

Drawdown & FIRE

  • Drawdown order: Cash first, then Investments, then Super
  • FIRE date: the first month where assets can sustain all projected expenses through to your nominated death age
  • Pre-super bridge: non-super assets must cover all expenses from retirement until preservation age — this is often the binding constraint for early retirees
  • Estate at death represents the residual super balance — super is drawn last and therefore compounds the longest
  • Retirement Readiness Analysis is a snapshot test using current assets only — it does not account for future salary growth that would be earned by continuing to work

Limitations

  • Assumes constant investment returns — does not model sequence-of-returns risk
  • Does not model capital gains tax on investment disposals
  • Does not model Age Pension eligibility, assets tests, or income tests
  • Does not model early super access (financial hardship, terminal illness, etc.)
  • Not financial advice — consult a licensed financial adviser before making decisions
